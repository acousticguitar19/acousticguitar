OUR FAVORITES IN ACOUSTIC GUITARS

Putting up 'best of' lists is always a fun since each instrument we show is absolutely wonderful in its own right. However, if we had to choose only one acoustic guitar to last us the rest of our lives, we'd probably go with the Martin D-28.

It's difficult not to identify The Beatles, Bob Dylan, and Elvis Presley with this lovely acoustic guitar while thinking about some of the greatest performers in music history. With greater bracing and a shortened neck, the modern-day version improves on older versions, but these adjustments simply serve to enhance what is music's ultimate acoustic workhorse.

From Bob Dylan to Billie Joe Armstrong, Woody Guthrie, and Myles Kennedy, the J-45 has been seen draped about the shoulders of many renowned guitarists throughout the years. The guitar was dubbed "The Workhorse" because of its modest appearance and folksy appeal, and it was considered as the working man's flattop. With a deep low-end and singing mid-range, it's always heard, regardless of the environment.

If you've been tracking the pricing of the Gibson J-45 over the past several years, you'll notice a significant spike - you won't get much change back from $/£2500 right now - and it doesn't seem that the prices will be going down any time soon.

Fortunately, Epiphone has been hard at work precisely reproducing the legendary sloping shoulder dreadnought - and at a far more reasonable price! Epiphone has gone out of its way to nail every detail and pay honor to the iconic acoustic, with an all-solid-wood construction, quarter-sawn spruce bracing, and tapered dovetail neck joint. Every detail, including the finish, has been meticulously examined. The thick plastic-like varnish has been replaced with a delicate and flexible aged finish that we enjoyed playing throughout our testing.
